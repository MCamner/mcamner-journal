# Writing Style — McAmner Journal

## Core principles

Short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph. No filler.

The prose does not explain what you can already see. It explains what is non-obvious — a constraint, a judgment, a reason.

Never announce what you are about to say. Say it.

---

## Sentence structure

Short sentences preferred. Longer sentences when rhythm requires it.

No bullet points in post body text. Prose only.

Fragment sentences are acceptable when they carry weight:

> "The terminal is not retro. It is honest."

> "Simple. Direct. Impossible to improve."

---

## Post structure

Every post follows the same pattern:

1. **Boot box header** — title + two-line subtitle. First line: factual. Second line: interpretive.
2. **Post box** — `>> title` heading, `film-intro` metadata line (optional), body paragraphs, optional `film-rating`
3. **Figure** — SVG robot decoration
4. **Journal box** — navigation commands

### Metadata line (`film-intro`)

Used for films, music, objects. Format:

```text
Artist · Album · Year · Personnel
Director · Film · Year · Country
```

### Rating line (`film-rating`)

Optional. Used for films and music. Examples:

- `Rating: essential`
- `Rating: major`
- `Rating: significant`

---

## Tone

- Direct. Never hedged.
- Observational. Not prescriptive.
- Interested in structure, restraint, what lasts.
- Occasionally dry. Not ironic.
- First-person when earned, not habitual.

Do not use:

- "I think that..."
- "In my opinion..."
- Marketing language
- Superlatives without evidence

---

## Subject approach

### On films

Focus on: structure, mood, restraint, what the film understands. Not plot summary.

Example opening:

> "A quiet study in loyalty, paranoia, and the cost of secrecy."

### On music

Focus on: what makes it work structurally. Energy as a choice, not decoration.

Example:

> "What Kill 'Em All understood before most of its contemporaries was that energy is a structural choice, not a decoration."

### On objects

Focus on: why it was made the way it was. Durability, intent, material honesty.

### On notes/projects

Focus on: the problem it solves, the principle behind it. Not feature lists.

Example:

> "Good software does not announce itself. It creates conditions for work and then disappears into the background."

---

## Headline style

Lowercase preferred for notes:

- `the machine room`
- `macos-scripts`
- `atlas-one`

Title case for films, music, objects, books:

- `Back in Black`
- `Le Samouraï`
- `Guitar Pro 8`

---

## Subheadings in post-box

Use `>> subject` format. Matches terminal aesthetic.

---

## Length

Posts are short. 4–8 paragraphs. No padding.

If a thought does not add something, cut it.
