# Stalker

*Film 006 · Dir. Andrei Tarkovsky · 1979*

Rating: essential

---

Somewhere beyond the military cordon is the Zone — a region where the laws of physics have stopped applying, where the landscape rearranges itself, where the rules change depending on who is walking through them. At the center of the Zone is a room. The room grants wishes. No one who has found it has used it.

A Stalker is a guide who leads people in. This one leads two men: a Writer, who has come looking for inspiration, and a Professor, who has come to understand. The three of them walk. The film is almost entirely that walk. No action. No revelation. Long takes over abandoned industrial water and corridors and grass growing through broken concrete.

Tarkovsky shot in a ruined hydroelectric plant in Estonia. The Zone looks like somewhere that was built for a purpose and then stopped. The landscape is specific and unexplainable at the same time — industrial without being a factory, ruined without being dramatic.

What the Zone is for depends on who is asking. The Writer decides inspiration is not worth having if it requires a wish. The Professor reveals he came to destroy the room entirely — he brought a bomb. The Stalker, who has organized his whole life around the Zone's sacredness, watches both of them and understands that the room has already done what it does. It reveals what you actually want, which is rarely what you came for.

The film does not explain the Zone. It does not explain why the room does what it does, or whether it works at all, or whether the Stalker's daughter has powers because of his proximity to the Zone or for some other reason. The questions are not answered because answering them would be beside the point.

Stalker is about faith and its relationship to evidence. The Stalker believes in the Zone without proof. The Writer and the Professor want proof before they believe in anything. Neither position is fully vindicated. The film ends where it started — in the grey world outside the Zone — and nothing has changed except the people.

That is what the Zone is for.
