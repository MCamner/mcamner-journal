Not by complexity, but by restraint.

>> structure

Structure is often mistaken for complexity.

But the strongest structures are usually quiet. They do not announce themselves. They hold things in place.

In infrastructure, structure is what makes systems understandable. In exhibitions, it is what lets the work breathe. In text, it is what gives thought a shape.

The point is not to add more. The point is to remove until the remaining parts carry weight.
